** Why BUSL and not AGPL
BUSL gives Bits the protection AGPL does not: a time-limited non-compete on
hosted services. AGPL only requires that you share your source if you modify
Bits and run it as a service — it does not prevent a competitor from doing
exactly that. BUSL preserves the copyleft endgame (each version converts to
AGPL after four years) while restricting hosted competition during the
protection window.

It is source-available.

* Commercial License
A commercial license is available for organisations that cannot comply with
BUSL-1.1. A commercial license lifts both restrictions:

- The SaaS non-compete that applies before the Change Date
- The AGPL-style copyleft that applies on or after the Change Date

Typical reasons to buy:

- You want to offer Bits (or build a service substantially based on Bits) to
  third parties on a hosted or embedded basis
- You incorporate Bits into a proprietary product or service and cannot meet
  AGPL's source-disclosure obligations
- Your legal or compliance posture requires terms that BUSL does not provide
